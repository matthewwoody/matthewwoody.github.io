Welcome!

Outside of school and work, I like to play hacky sack as well as work out/weightlift in general and am always on the lookout for new Middle Eastern food spots (thank you to Khan's Halal Cart outside of Jamaica Station for inspiring this in me- best chicken over rice of all time).
Passively, I'm a big time enjoyer of 90's and 2000's animated shows! If you're in the mood to pick up a new show, I absolutely recommend Batman: The Animated Series, followed by Batman Beyond; both of which are available for free on Internet Archive. Starting Off

I started out my high education journey as a business major- intending to never take another math class again! This mindset quickly collapsed during my first semester at Santa Rosa Junior College, where I was taking a finite math class and found it much more interesting than my business and econmics classes. The following semester, I switched my major to mathematics and went through the trials and tribulations of calculus, in addition to some computer science and physics.

Figuring It Out

I think the words of Charles De Mar from Better Off Dead more or less describe my sentiment during this time- "I've been at this high school for seven and a half years- and I'm no dummy!"
Essentially, after a couple semesters of classes and work at my part-time retail job, I began to crave something more for myself and started applying to some summer internships just to see what was out there. I ended up working for my local city government, helping optimize their water use billing system and catching some unnoticed backflows costing people money and the city water. I also helped out with their water conservation programs, spreading awareness about the rebates and benefits available to participants. It was a solid first internship experience and helped me realize I wanted to apply my technical knowledge to the environment. After that summer, I'd discovered that since I'd switched my major, I was off of the ideal community college transfer timeline and was left with a spring semester in which I could either take one more math class or try my hand at another internship, this time armed with more knowledge and desire to apply myself in Earth Science.

Long Island or Bust

Wouldn't you know it! One of those spring internships panned out after all, and I moved across the country for 10 weeks to work in the Cloud Processes and Measurement Group at Brookhaven National Lab where I worked with XSACR data from ARM's 2021-2022 TRACER field campaign In summary, I used this data to "cluster" the Houston Bay Breeze inside of an Orienteering Tape Recorder diagram and extracted relevant statistics such as front velocity, range and average wind speed. This was a really transformative experience, and I left knowing that Atmospheric Science was the field for me.


What do you know, back again! The summer of 2025 was truly one for the books, I was invited by the internship program staff to help administer the summer internship, since I'd chatted with them a lot when I'd pick up my stipend the previous semester and they'd thought I'd be a good fit for the role- helping them host events (bbq's, game nights, etc), be a go-between and be there for the interns in general. This was an absolutely incredible experience and I was able to get familiar with all types of the work being done at BNL, meet great people and build a network.

Sonoma State

Finally the time came for me to transfer to a 4-year school, and after having lived on my own since the start of the year, my heart wasn't in packing up and moving again to attend school, so I decided to stay in the neighborhood and go to my local state school. This was definitely the right choice for me, I enjoy the smaller class sizes and relationships I've made with my professors- I'm definitely thriving here and am able to build my future into something I want.
